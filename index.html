Product Tracks

Each product inside the Trivox ecosystem targets a different layer of the same problem: making practical software and production workflows easier to build, operate, and ship.

What it is: A Python-first arcade framework and product line for building small playable games, validating runtime architecture, and testing shipping workflows.

What problem it solves: It reduces the overhead of prototyping mechanics, reusing engine patterns, and turning experiments into real releases.

Who it is for: Developers and technical creators working on lightweight games, gameplay prototypes, and reusable game-tech foundations.

Ashframe Workflow System

What it is: A systems-oriented product track for structuring project architecture, production flows, and repeatable workflow foundations across creative and technical work.

What problem it solves: It tackles setup chaos, inconsistent processes, and the friction that slows teams down between idea, implementation, and delivery.

Who it is for: Solo builders and small teams that need a clearer operating layer for production without adopting heavyweight studio infrastructure.

llmix AI Workflow Utility

What it is: An AI-oriented utility layer for composing model-powered tasks into practical development and creative workflows instead of isolated prompt experiments.

What problem it solves: It reduces prompt sprawl, context handoff friction, and the repetition that shows up when AI is used without a system around it.

Who it is for: Developers and technical creators who want AI to behave like part of the toolchain, not a detached assistant tab.
